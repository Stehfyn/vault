# Every Programmer Should Know List

Index of "things every programmer should know" — pitched at the level of foundational concepts and famous papers rather than tutorials: floating-point (Goldberg's "What Every Computer Scientist..."), Unicode (Spolsky's "Absolute Minimum"), latency numbers, time/timezones, security basics, distributed systems fallacies, the Cap-Theorem and related lore, regex, big-O, hashing. Useful as a checklist of the canon when mentoring or designing an interview loop. Quality is uneven — some entries are blog posts, others are seminal papers — so curate as you go.

Fallacies of Distributed Computing, Latency Numbers Every Programmer Should
Know, and the Two Generals / Byzantine Generals problems. If a candidate or
junior hasn't read at least these, that's the gap to fill.
